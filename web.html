Church for all Nations



City: Jerusalem


Hotel: Ramada Hotel

The Church of All Nations (also known as the Basilica of the Agony) in Jerusalem. It is located on the Mount of Olives and commemorates the site where Jesus prayed before his arrest. The church was built between 1919 and 1924 using funds donated from many different countries. The coat-of-arms of twelve of the countries are incorporated into the ceiling, small dome, and also into the interior mosaics.

The Great Omari Mosque


City: Gaza


Hotel: Roots Hotel

The Great Omari Mosque, located in Bethlehem, is a significant religious and historical site. Constructed in the 12th century during the Crusades, it was built on the site of a Byzantine church dedicated to St. Mary. Named after Caliph Umar ibn al-Khattab, who is known for his role , the mosque symbolizes the Islamic heritage of the region. Its architectural design features classic Islamic elements, including a spacious prayer hall and a prominent minaret, reflecting the styles typical of the era.

Church of the Nativity



City: Bethlehem


Hotel: Bayt Ateeq Hotel

The Church of the Nativity is one of the oldest continuously inhabited Christian churches in the world and holds great significance as the traditional birthplace of Jesus Christ. Located in Bethlehem, West Bank, it is a major pilgrimage site for Christians and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The church was originally commissioned by Constantine the Great a short time after his mother Helena's visit to Jerusalem and Bethlehem in 325–326, on the site that was traditionally considered to be the birthplace of Jesus.

Dome of the Rock



City: Jerusalem


Hotel: Cesar Premier Hotel

The Dome of the Rock is an iconic Islamic shrine located on the Temple Mount in Jerusalem. Completed in 691 CE, it is one of the oldest extant examples of Islamic architecture. The structure features a distinctive gold-plated dome, which rises prominently over the city skyline.The interior of the dome is lavishly decorated with mosaic, faience and marble, much of which was added several centuries after its completion. It also contains Qur'anic inscriptions.

City: Al Khalil

This is the Ibrahimi Mosque (or Cave of the Patriarchs) in Hebron. It is a significant religious site in Islam, Christianity, and Judaism, believed to be the burial place of the prophets Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob. The rectangular stone enclosure lies on a northwest–southeast axis, and is divided into two sections by a wall running between the northwestern three fifths, and the southeastern two fifths.

Al-Aqsa Mosque



City: Jerusalem


Hotel: Cesar Premier Hotel

Al-Aqsa Mosque, located in the Old City of Jerusalem, is considered the third holiest site in Islam after the Kaaba in Mecca and the Prophet's Mosque in Medina. It is believed to be the place where the Prophet Muhammad was transported during (Al Isra and Mi'raj) to the heavens. Before the Kaaba was designated as the qibla , Muslims originally faced Al-Aqsa during their prayers.
